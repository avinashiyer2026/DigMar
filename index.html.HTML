Root Cause & Strategy

Analysis & Improvement Roadmap

Understanding why the numbers shifted and identifying clear actions to address them.

The Visibility vs. Click Gap

While the site appears more frequently in search results, the Click-Through Rate fell from 0.6% to 0.4%. Higher impressions signal improving authority, but meta titles and descriptions need stronger calls-to-action to convert visibility into visits.

LinkedIn: Engagement & Follower Trend

February saw one fewer post and followers dropped by more than half. Analyzing January's top-performing content may help replicate that engagement spike in coming months.

Action Plan

4 Strategic Recommendations

Priority actions to recover and accelerate growth in March 2026.

1

Optimize for CTR

Evaluate meta titles and descriptions for keywords generating high impressions but low clicks. Use compelling, benefit-driven language and clear calls to action.

2

Content Enhancement

For the 9 keywords on Page 1, enrich content depth and quality to secure positions and prevent ranking regression as competition intensifies.

3

Competitor & AEO Analysis

Assess competitors ranking higher for AEO keywords. Identify content gaps, featured snippet opportunities, and structural improvements.

4

Refine LinkedIn Strategy

Analyze January's high-performing posts (7.6% engagement) to identify what formats drove results. Replicate that formula to rebuild follower growth momentum.

Key Takeaway for March 2026

February's data tells a story of growing visibility paired with a conversion gap. The foundation is strengthening — impressions are up, rankings are improving, and authority is holding — but the next priority is translating that visibility into clicks, sessions, and engaged followers. The strategy above directly addresses each dimension of this gap.
